Appendix · The Research

Why This
ICP

The evidence behind the wedge: why "AI-prototype to production, fintech first" is a rising tide and not a one-off — and what we honestly can't measure.

The short version
What the research says

The tools feeding our business are growing about as fast as any software category ever recorded. The pain we fix — fast-built code that isn't production-grade — is documented by independent academics, not just vendors. The buyers are numerous and funded.

The catch: we're not early. The "rescue" and "AI training" niches are filling fast, mostly with solo freelancers. So the demand is validated — it's our positioning (operators who've scaled startups, senior judgment, fintech depth) that has to win.

Proof point 1 · The faucet

The Upstream Is Exploding

Every app built fast with AI or no-code is a future "make it real" job. That pool is growing at a near-unprecedented pace.

$0→$100M
Lovable's ARR in its first 8 months — among the fastest ever; ~$400M ARR and a $6.6B valuation by early 2026.
Source: TechCrunch, Bloomberg
$1B ARR
Cursor's annual run-rate by Nov 2025 (from $100M in Jan), valued at $29.3B in its $2.3B Series D.
Source: Cursor, Business Wire
~50%
Share of all global venture funding that went to AI in 2025 (~$211B) — overwhelmingly software-first companies.
Source: Crunchbase

Add Replit (~$10M→~$300M ARR in 2025, 50M+ users), Bolt (0→~$40M ARR in ~5 months), and the broader low-code market (~$25–35B, growing ~20–25%/yr; Gartner projected 70% of new apps would use low-code/no-code by 2025). The trend is unambiguous. (Replit/Bolt ARR via Sacra estimates; Gartner via secondary sources.)

Proof point 2 · The pain

Fast-Built Code Isn't Production-Grade

This is the part skeptics dismiss as hype — so note that the strongest evidence is from academics and independent researchers, not vendors with something to sell.

40–50%
Of AI-generated code contains vulnerabilities under security-relevant conditions — and newer models aren't safer.
Source: NYU, Georgetown CSET, Veracode
66%
Of developers say their #1 frustration is AI output that's "almost right, but not quite" — debugging it is the #2 (45%). And more distrust AI accuracy (46%) than trust it (33%).
Source: Stack Overflow 2025
2,000+
Vulnerabilities — plus 400+ exposed secrets — found across ~5,600 publicly deployed vibe-coded apps. Separately, Wiz found 1 in 5 organizations on these platforms exposed to risk.
Source: Escape, Wiz

Reinforced by DORA/Google (a 25% rise in AI adoption tracked with a ~7.2% drop in delivery stability) and named 2025 incidents — Replit's AI agent deleting a live production database, the Base44 auth-bypass. The deeper, more dangerous flaws (auth, architecture, secrets) are exactly the senior-judgment work the tools and junior teams do worst.

Proof point 4 · The catch

We're Not Early

The honest counterweight: the niche is filling fast — which is why positioning, not market existence, is the real question.

Source: IKANGAI (citing 404 Media), CodeConductor

Read of the competitive landscape

The opening: senior operators who've actually scaled startups to profitability — not cleanup freelancers or junior shops. With fintech depth on top, that's a position the crowded low end can't occupy.

Intellectual honesty

What We Can't Measure

  • The conversion rate from "uses Lovable/Cursor" to "pays a senior firm to harden it." No data exists — it's the real unknown. Plenty of this work goes to cheap freelancers or never gets done.
  • Durability of the rescue niche. If the tools get materially better at production-readiness, the work shifts toward enablement and architecture and away from cleanup. The two-pronged wedge partly hedges this.
  • Success Academy is a sample of one (and not closed yet). This is a strong, well-supported hypothesis to test with real outreach — not proof.
  • Private ARR figures are estimates, and some analyst numbers are quoted secondhand. The independent academic sources on code quality are what make the pain thesis credible.
Connecting evidence to the ICP

So Why These Three Filters

Domain · Fintech first

Regulated, high-stakes domains are where bad AI code is most dangerous and most expensive — so buyers won't shop on hourly rate. Plus we have real reps (LoanCAD). It's where our edge is unfair.

Stage · Funded

The funded pool (55k+ companies, ~3,600 seed deals/yr) is large, software-first, and has budget and urgency — the two things the research says we can't manufacture.

Trigger · Built fast

The exploding upstream plus the documented "last 30%" gap means the "built it fast, now it has to be real" trigger is common, growing, and acute — exactly where senior judgment beats the freelancer crowd.
